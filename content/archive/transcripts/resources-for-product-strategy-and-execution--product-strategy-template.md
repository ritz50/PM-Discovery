Product Strategy Template
<aside> 🚧 Use this template to kick-start your product strategy. Remember to include/exclude sections based on your unique situation.
</aside>
1. Vision and Goals:
Vision: [Describe the long-term vision for your product, including its intended impact and purpose.]
Goals: [List specific, measurable goals your product should achieve. E.g., Increase user base to 100,000 within 12 months.]
2. Value Proposition:
Unique Value Proposition: [Explain what makes your product unique and why customers should choose it over competitors.]
3. Target Market:
Target Market: [Identify the primary target market or customer segment.]
Needs: [List the key needs, pain points, or preferences of your target customers.]
4. Competitive Analysis:
Strengths: [Identify your product's strengths compared to competitors.]
Weaknesses: [Highlight areas where your product needs improvement or faces challenges.]
Opportunities: [Identify market opportunities or trends your product can capitalize on.]
Threats: [List potential threats or challenges from the competition or market changes.]
5. Feature Prioritization:
Key Features: [List the features and functionalities required for the product.]
Prioritization: [Explain how you prioritize these features based on user value, technical feasibility, and market demand.]
6. Roadmap:
Roadmap Timeline: [Create a visual roadmap outlining feature development and release schedule.]
Key Milestones: [Specify the key milestones for each quarter or release.]
7. Resource Allocation:
Budget: [Determine the budget required for development, marketing, and other expenses.]
Personnel: [Define the roles and number of personnel needed for development, testing, marketing, etc.]
8. Metrics and KPIs:
KPIs: [List the key performance indicators and metrics used to measure success.]
Targets: [Set specific targets or benchmarks for each KPI. E.g., Achieve a customer satisfaction rating of 4.5/5.]
9. Lifecycle Management:
Product Evolution: [Describe how the product will evolve over time, including updates, maintenance, and potential retirement.]
EOL Plan: [Include an End-of-Life (EOL) plan for when the product is phased out.]
10. Risk Assessment:
Risks: [Identify potential risks and challenges that could impact the product's success.]
Mitigation: [Detail mitigation plans and strategies to address these risks.]
11. Communication and Alignment:
Stakeholders: [List key stakeholders who need to be informed about or involved in the product strategy.]
Communication Plan: [Describe how you will communicate the strategy to the team and stakeholders.]
12. Iterative Adaptation:
Adaptability: [Highlight the product's flexibility and the ability to adapt based on user feedback and market changes.]
13. Review and Update:
Review Schedule: [Define how and when the product strategy will be reviewed and updated.]
Triggers for Update: [Specify what conditions or events trigger a strategy update.]
