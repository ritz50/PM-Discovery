# Rubric — Ride-Sharing Case

## Strong answer includes

- Problem statement focused on underserved segment or pain (not "build an app")
- Assumption map with top 3 risky assumptions
- Discovery plan: who to interview, what to learn, success criteria
- Bottom-up sizing attempt (even rough)
- Clear recommendation with tradeoffs

## Red flags

- Jumping to features without validation plan
- Ignoring incumbent dynamics
- No mention of differentiation or why you'd win

## Model answer outline

Frame opportunity around a specific wedge — e.g. reliability for airport commuters. Map assumptions: willingness to switch, unit economics, driver supply. Plan: 15 switch interviews, wait-time diary study, LOI from 2 corporate accounts. Size SAM for airport segment only. Recommend pursue wedge with 4-week experiment (landing page + concierge rides) before full build.
