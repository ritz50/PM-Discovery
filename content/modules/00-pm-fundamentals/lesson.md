# PM Fundamentals (helloPM)

*Source: PM Fundamentals Session 1 & 2 — Ankit Shukla, helloPM cohort*

## Purpose of this module

This is the **index of the whole program** — it introduces keywords and the big picture before deep dives in later modules. It is normal to feel overwhelmed here; details come in Discovery, Research, and Strategy modules.

## The 3Ds: Product Management Lifecycle

Every company follows a similar process:

1. **Product Discovery** — Find what users value and what the business should build
2. **Product Delivery** — Plan, specify, and ship (roadmap, PRD, backlog, agile execution)
3. **Product Distribution** — Get the product to users and drive adoption (growth, GTM)

**Where PMs have the biggest leverage:** Discovery. If you find the right problems and opportunities, delivery and distribution become easier.

### Delivery has two parts

- **Clarity of what to do** — PM-owned (requirements, prioritization, roadmap grooming)
- **Building and designing** — Engineering and design-owned

### Full-stack PM mindset

Aim to understand all three Ds even if your day-to-day skews toward delivery (common for Product Owners). Business/growth PMs often lean discovery + distribution.

## Product sense & user centricity

Products succeed when they **address a desire or solve an important problem** in the user's life. The world cares about what your product does for them — not your aspirations alone.

**Exercise:** Regularly ask — what job is the user hiring this product to do?

## Converting ambiguity to clarity

A core PM job (especially in Strategy & Execution): take fuzzy goals and turn them into plans the team can execute — vision → strategy → roadmap → PRD → stories.
