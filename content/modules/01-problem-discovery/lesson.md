# Problem Discovery

*Enriched from helloPM Product Discovery & Research Session 1 + PM Fundamentals*

Problem discovery is the foundation of product management.

> In product discovery, the PM's purpose is to find what users will value — through user research, market research, Mom Test, JTBD, interviews, personas, and journey maps — so you build something users want and the business moves forward. (helloPM Strategy S3) Before building, you must validate that a problem is **real**, **painful**, and **worth solving**.

## Why it matters

Teams that skip problem discovery build features nobody wants. Discovery reduces waste by testing assumptions cheaply before engineering commitment.

## Core activities

1. **Frame the problem** — Write a clear problem statement tied to a specific user segment
2. **Map assumptions** — List beliefs about users, problems, and solutions; rank by risk
3. **Validate** — Run interviews, surveys, or experiments to test top assumptions
4. **Synthesize** — Turn findings into an opportunity assessment

## Problem statement template

> [User segment] struggles to [job/task] because [root cause], which results in [negative outcome].

Good problem statements are specific, user-centered, and free of solution language.

## Assumption mapping

Plot assumptions on two axes:
- **Importance** — How critical if this assumption is wrong?
- **Uncertainty** — How little evidence do we have?

Focus validation on **high importance + high uncertainty** assumptions first.

## Problem-solution fit signals

| Signal | Strong fit | Weak fit |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| User pain | Active workarounds exist | Nice-to-have |
| Willingness | Users pay or invest time | Lukewarm interest |
| Frequency | Daily/weekly pain | Once a year |
| Market | Growing segment | Shrinking |

## Discovery brief

A discovery brief aligns the team on:
- Target user and problem hypothesis
- Key assumptions to test
- Research plan and timeline
- Success criteria for moving forward

## Common mistakes

- Leading with solutions ("We should build an AI chatbot")
- Talking to only friendly users
- Confusing opinions with evidence
- Stopping discovery after one round of interviews

## Key takeaway

Discovery is not a phase you finish — it is a continuous habit of testing what you believe about users and problems.
