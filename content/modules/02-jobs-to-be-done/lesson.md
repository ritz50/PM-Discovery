# Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)

JTBD shifts focus from **who** the customer is to **what progress** they are trying to make. People "hire" products to get a job done.

## The core idea (helloPM)

> When customers buy a product, they are hiring it to make progress in a specific circumstance.

> People do not care about your product. They care about getting a job done. Products change; jobs often stay stable. (helloPM Discovery S1)

### Three job types (helloPM)

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Functional** | Get from A to B, complete a task |
| **Emotional** | Feel confident, reduce anxiety |
| **Social** | Signal status, fit in with peers |

Jobs depend on **context** (time, money, location, constraints).

Demographics tell you who someone is. Jobs tell you **why** they act.

## Job statement format

> When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome].

Example: *When I'm preparing for a PM interview, I want to practice articulating frameworks, so I can demonstrate structured thinking.*

## Four Forces of Progress

When someone considers switching to a new solution, four forces act:

| Force | Direction | Question |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Push** | Toward change | What's broken about the current situation? |
| **Pull** | Toward change | What's attractive about the new solution? |
| **Anxiety** | Against change | What worries them about switching? |
| **Habit** | Against change | What keeps them tied to the status quo? |

Switch happens when **Push + Pull > Anxiety + Habit**.

## Switch interviews

The best JTBD research explores a **past purchase or adoption decision**:

1. First thought — When did they first consider a change?
2. Passive looking — What triggered active search?
3. Active looking — What options did they evaluate?
4. Deciding — What tipped the decision?
5. Consuming — How did onboarding go?

## JTBD vs personas

| Personas | JTBD |
|----------|------|
| Who is the user? | What progress do they seek? |
| Demographic segments | Circumstance-based |
| Risk: stereotypes | Risk: abstract without interviews |

Use both: personas for empathy, JTBD for motivation.

## Application in discovery

- Frame interview questions around past behavior, not hypotheticals
- Map forces before designing solutions
- Identify anxieties your product must address
- Find non-obvious competitors (what else gets "hired")

## Key takeaway

Don't ask "Would you use this?" Ask "Tell me about the last time you tried to solve this."
