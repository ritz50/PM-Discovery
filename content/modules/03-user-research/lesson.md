# User Research Methods

*Source: helloPM Product Discovery & Research — Sessions 1 & 2 (Ankit Shukla)*

Choose methods based on your discovery question, not convenience.

## helloPM session agenda

1. Need for user research — why startups fail (solution-first, weak research)
2. Methods to uncover problems: people problems, **Jobs to Be Done**, **Mom Test**
3. Conducting research: finding users, asking right questions, building **personas**, **empathy maps**, **journey maps**
4. Situational examples, interview questions, competitive research, end-to-end AI product case study
5. Session 2: **Lovable.dev** product case study (modern AI/no-code discovery)

## Qual vs quant

| Qualitative | Quantitative |
|-------------|--------------|
| Why, how, motivations | How many, how often |
| Interviews, observation | Surveys, analytics |
| Small samples, deep | Large samples, broad |

## Method selection

- **Exploratory** → interviews, diary studies
- **Evaluative** → usability tests, concept tests
- **Generative** → contextual inquiry, JTBD interviews

## Interview best practices

1. Ask about past behavior, not future hypotheticals
2. Use open-ended questions
3. Follow the energy — probe interesting threads
4. Silence is your friend
5. Synthesize within 24 hours

## Research synthesis

Affinity mapping: cluster observations → identify themes → extract insights → link to opportunities.

## Key takeaway

The method serves the question. Start with what you need to learn, then pick how to learn it.
