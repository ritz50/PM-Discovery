# Prioritization & Strategy

*Source: helloPM Product Strategy and Execution — Sessions 1–3*

Discovery generates more opportunities than you can build. Prioritization is how you choose.

## PM process (helloPM Strategy module)

1. **PM process overview** — Discovery → Delivery → Distribution; convert ambiguity to clarity
2. **Product roadmap** — Vision, strategy, design thinking, OKRs, prioritization → roadmap
3. **Product execution** — PRD, agile, stakeholder management, best practices

## Product strategy = 3C + P (helloPM S2)

| Component | Focus |
|-----------|--------|
| **Customers** | Who, needs, segments |
| **Competition** | Alternatives, positioning |
| **Company** | Capabilities, regulations, trends (e.g. India Stack) |
| **Product** | Features, 4Ps (Product, Place, Price, Promotion) |

Outcome of strategy → **well-designed product roadmap** → **PRD** → execution.

## Roadmap purpose (helloPM S3)

- **Alignment** — team and stakeholders know what's coming
- **Clarity** — convert research into planned initiatives
- **Communication** — some B2B companies publish roadmaps publicly

Steps: define initiatives → **prioritize and sequence** → timeline view

## RICE recap

Score = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) ÷ Effort. Use for comparing initiatives.

## OST recap

Connect outcomes → opportunities → solutions → experiments. Use for continuous discovery.

## Outcome vs output

- **Output** — Feature shipped
- **Outcome** — User or business behavior changed

Prioritize by outcomes, not feature lists.

## Communicating tradeoffs

When saying no: name the opportunity cost, reference evidence, propose what you ARE doing instead.

## Key takeaway

Prioritization is a conversation, not a spreadsheet. Scores inform; judgment decides.
